Who is a Seafarer?

A seafarer is a professional who works aboard a ship, playing a vital role in maritime operations. Seafarers include deck officers, marine engineers, electro-technical officers (ETOs), ratings, and catering staff, among others. They ensure the safe navigation, operation, and maintenance of vessels that transport goods, passengers, or perform specialized tasks like fishing and offshore services..

The Journey to Become Master Mariner Begins Here.

   Stages:

  • To begin with, go for your Mandatory and pursue sea-time experience; Seek Cadetship Placement in any shipping company where you could be given an opportunity to serve onboard as a Deck or Engine Cadet, for complete uninterrupted 12 months sea time.

  • Seek Eligibility from IMO recognized Flag administration to study and write your competency certification in their affiliated maritime Academy/Institution, where you will earn your first Officer‚Äôs Competency Certification (COC) OOW Deck or Engine.

  • Pass Your OOW (Unlimited) Certificate of Competency (CoC) exam. This allows you to serve as a third officer on ships of 500 GT or more, in unlimited Sea area. You must also meet STCW Section A-II/1 standards.

  • Advance to Chief Mate: Accumulate at least 12 months of sea service as OOW. Complete advanced training in navigation, ship handling, cargo operations, and leadership. Pass the Chief Mate (Unlimited) CoC exam (STCW Section A-II/2).
    This Qualify you to work as 2nd or Chief Officer of unlimited size and sea area.

  • Qualify as Master Mariner (Unlimited): After you must have accumulated at least 36 months of sea service, including time as Chief Mate. Complete Master-level training in ship management, maritime law, and emergency response. Pass the Master (Unlimited) CoC exam under STCW Section A-II/2.

  • Maintain Certification and Compliance: Undergo periodic refresher training and medical exams, Stay compliant with flag state and port state control requirements. Keep your Continuous Discharge Certificate (CDC) and other documents up to date.

Pathway For Student Without Science Based Background To Become Captain or Chief Engineer.

For a student without a science-based background who aspire to become a Captain or Chief Engineer, up to Master Mariner ‚ÄúNo hope is lost‚Äù NIMASA provides alternative routes to climb the certification Ladder. Here‚Äôs a structured pathway tailored to Nigerian maritime standards, this may be slightly different in other Administration or Flag State:



Meet Basic Entry Requirements.

  • Minimum qualification: Secondary school certificate (SSCE/WAEC/NECO). Must be medically fit as per maritime health standards. Age requirement: Typically 18 years and above.
  • Begin with a General Maritime Entry Program
    Enroll in a General Purpose (GP) Rating Course at an accredited maritime training institution in Nigeria. This course typically lasts 6 months and does not require a science background. Covers basic seamanship, safety procedures, engine room operations, and navigation fundamentals.
  • Gain Sea Experience as a Rating
    After completing GP Rating, join a vessel as a Deck or Engine Rating. Accumulate sea service experience, which is crucial for career progression. Work under the supervision of officers to gain practical knowledge.
  • Upgrade to Officer-Level Training
    After gaining sufficient sea time, enroll in a Near Coastal Voyage (NCV) Associate Course.
    This course is designed for those without formal science education but with practical experience.
    You may need to pass an aptitude test or bridge course to qualify.
  • Obtain STCW Certifications
    Complete mandatory STCW training (firefighting, survival, first aid, etc.). Seek eligibility From NIMASA and proceed to Accredited Preparatory training Institution, in preparation for OOW Examination, usually last six months. Pass and Obtain an Officer of the Watch (OOW) Near Coastal Voyage (NCV) Certificate for deck officers or an Engine Room Watchkeeping Certificate for engineers. With this you can serve as 3rd or 2nd Officer depending on your experience on Vessel >3000GT (NCV)
  • Advance to Chief Mate >3,000GT (NCV):
    Accumulate at least 12 months of sea service as OOW. Complete advanced training in navigation, ship handling, cargo operations, and leadership. Pass the Chief Mate (NCV) CoC exam (STCW Section A-II/2). This Qualify you to work as 2nd or Chief Officer of unlimited size and Limited sea area (NCV). This Certificate also qualify you to work as a Master of Vessel <3000GT Near Coastal Voyages (NCV)
  • Qualify as Master/Chief Engineer of Vessel >3000GT/KWT (NCV):
    After you must have accumulated at least 36 months of sea service, including time as Chief Mate or 2nd Engr onboard NCV Ship. Complete Master/Chief Engr-level training in ship management, maritime law, and emergency response.
  • Pass the NCV Master or Chief Engineer Certificate of Competency (CoC) exam under STCW Section A-II/2, and NIMASA regulations. This certification allows you to command or manage vessels >3000GT operating within Nigerian and West African coastal waters.
  • Continue Professional Development;
    Gain additional certifications in ship handling, maritime law, and vessel management. At this stage you may Consider upgrading to Unlimited Master Mariner or Chief Engineer qualifications for international voyages.

Pathway For a Seafarer who is Unable to further his Education Beyond O-Level and Wish to Become Captain or Chief Engnr.

To obtain Deck or Engine Room Rating Watchkeeping Certification in Nigeria under NIMASA (Nigerian Maritime Administration and Safety Agency), candidates must follow a structured pathway that aligns with STCW (Standards of Training, Certification, and Watchkeeping) regulations. Here‚Äôs a step-by-step guide:

  • 1.Meet Basic Entry Requirements Minimum qualification: Secondary school certificate (SSCE/WAEC/NECO). Must be medically fit as per maritime health standards.Age requirement: Typically 18 years and above.
  • 2.Enroll in an Approved Maritime Training Institution Choose a NIMASA-accredited maritime academy offering Deck or Engine Room Rating courses. Some recognized institutions include: Maritime Academy of Nigeria (MAN), Oron Federal College of Fisheries and Marine Technology (FCFMT) Other NIMASA-approved training centers
  • 3.Complete Mandatory STCW Basic Safety Training This includes: Personal Survival Techniques Fire Prevention and Firefighting Elementary First Aid Personal Safety and Social Responsibility These courses ensure compliance with international maritime safety standards.
  • 4.Undergo Specialized Deck or Engine Room Rating Training Deck Rating Training covers: Basic seamanship, cargo handling, mooring operations. Navigation watchkeeping procedures. Use of maritime communication systems. Engine Room Rating Training covers: Machinery operations and maintenance. Engine room watchkeeping duties. Safety procedures for engine room operations.
  • 5.Apply for Seafarer‚Äôs Identification & Record Book (SIRB) This document is issued by NIMASA and is required for employment onboard vessels. It serves as an official record of sea service and qualifications.
  • 6.Gain Sea Service Experience Candidates must complete approved seagoing service under supervision. Minimum 12 months of onboard training is required. Training must be documented in a Seafarer‚Äôs Training Record Book.
  • 7.Obtain Watchkeeping Certification After completing training and sea service, candidates must: Pass NIMASA-approved competency assessments. Obtain the Ratings Forming Part of a Watch (Deck or Engine Room) Certificate. Certification aligns with STCW Section A-II/4 (Deck) and A-III/4 (Engine Room)
  • 8.Upgrade to Officer-Level Training After gaining sufficient sea time, Minimumjm 36 months, enroll in a Near Coastal Voyage (NCV) Associate Course. This course is designed for those without formal education but with practical experience. You may need to pass an aptitude test or bridge course to qualify.
  • 9.Continue Career Progression After gaining experience, ratings can: Upgrade to Officer of the Watch (OOW) certification. Pursue further maritime studies for higher ranks (e.g., Chief Engineer or Captain). Specialize in areas like electro-technical ratings or catering services.
